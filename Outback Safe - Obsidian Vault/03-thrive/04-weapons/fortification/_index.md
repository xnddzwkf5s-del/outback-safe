# Fortification

Fortification is defense in depth. Your group's safety depends not on a single wall but on layers of obstacles, detection, and defensive capability. A well-fortified position deters attack before it happens — raiders and hostile groups will choose softer targets if yours looks like too much trouble.

## Why Fortify?

1. **Deterrence** — Visible defenses reduce the likelihood of attack
2. **Delay** — Layers of obstacles slow an attacker, giving you time to respond
3. **Force multiplication** — Two defenders behind a wall can hold off twenty attackers
4. **Safe storage** — Your food, water, tools, weapons, and powder are behind walls
5. **Psychological security** — Your group sleeps better behind walls

## Defense in Depth

The principle: never rely on a single layer of defense.

```
    OUTER PERIMETER
    ┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
    │  Early warning (trip lines, noise   │
    │  makers, lookout posts)             │
    ├─────────────────────────────────────┤
    │  Obstacles (abatis, caltrops,       │
    │  thorn hedges, wire)                │
    ├─────────────────────────────────────┤
    │  Outer wall / palisade              │
    ├─────────────────────────────────────┤
    │  Killing ground (clear space        │
    │  20–30 m inside outer wall)         │
    ├─────────────────────────────────────┤
    │  Inner wall / keep                  │
    ├─────────────────────────────────────┤
    │  Final redoubt (secure room,        │
    │  cellar, last-stand position)       │
    └─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Each layer:
- **Warns** you of approaching threats
- **Slows** the attacker's advance
- **Channels** attackers into predictable paths (covered by your defensive positions)
- **Exhausts** the attacker's energy and morale before they reach your main defense

## Site Selection Before Construction

Before building any wall, choose the right location. See the Site Selection page for detailed criteria, but the essentials:

- **High ground** — Fighting uphill is exhausting; fighting downhill is easier
- **Water source inside the perimeter** — A siege that cuts off your water supply is unwinnable
- **Clear fields of fire** — No dead ground (areas an attacker can approach unseen)
- **Escape route** — A path out that you control. Fortresses become tombs without one
- **Defensible terrain** — Natural barriers (cliffs, rivers, swamps) reduce the wall length you must build
