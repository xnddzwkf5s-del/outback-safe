# Storing Gunpowder

Improper storage kills. A powder magazine that catches fire becomes a bomb. Design your storage to contain a fire, not to survive an explosion вҖ” because nothing survives a magazine explosion.

## Storage Principles

1. **Separate from living areas** вҖ” Magazine must be a dedicated structure at safe distance
2. **Keep dry** вҖ” Moisture ruins powder; dry powder is what you want but must be protected
3. **Limit quantities** вҖ” Never store more than you can afford to lose
4. **Separate from ignition sources** вҖ” No flames, sparks, or heat within 15 m
5. **Secure from unauthorised access** вҖ” Curious children, untrained group members, and intruders

## Magazine Design

### Distances

| Quantity Stored | Distance from Dwellings | Distance from Workshops |
|-----------------|------------------------|------------------------|
| < 1 kg | 10 m | 5 m |
| 1вҖ“5 kg | 25 m | 15 m |
| 5вҖ“20 kg | 50 m | 25 m |
| 20вҖ“50 kg | 100 m | 50 m |
| > 50 kg | 200 m minimum | 100 m minimum |

These are minimums. Double them if terrain allows. A magazine explosion throws burning debris hundreds of metres.

**Key features:**

- **Earth-bermed walls** вҖ” Earth absorbs blast upward, not outward
- **Light or frangible roof** вҖ” Designed to blow off first, directing blast upward (not through walls toward people)
- **Lightning rod** вҖ” Essential. Powder magazines struck by lightning are legendary disasters
- **Blast wall** вҖ” A freestanding wall 2 m in front of the door. Deflects any blast from the doorway upward
- **Ventilation** вҖ” Small, screened vents high and low to prevent moisture buildup. Vermin-proof mesh
- **Wooden floor or duckboards** вҖ” Never a bare earth floor (damp). Wood with air gap beneath
- **No iron or steel in construction** вҖ” Wooden pegs, copper nails, brass hinges. Every metal part a potential spark source

### Alternative: Buried Storage

For very small quantities (< 2 kg), a buried container is simpler:

{% steps() %}
Place powder in a sealed, watertight container вҖ” A glazed ceramic jar with a waxed cork, or a modern PVC pipe with threaded caps.;
Wrap in multiple layers of oiled cloth вҖ” Extra moisture barrier.;
Place inside a second container вҖ” Double-containment.;
Bury at least 0.5 m deep вҖ” In well-drained soil, away from water courses.;
Mark the location clearly вҖ” A stone cairn or distinctive marker. You do not want to forget where it is.;
Do not bury near trees вҖ” Roots will eventually breach the container.
{% end %}

## Container Types

| Container | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|-----------|------|------|----------|
| **Wooden keg** (oak, ash) | Traditional, breathes slightly | Not airtight; can absorb moisture in very humid climates | Magazines in temperate climates |
| **Glazed ceramic jar** | Airtight if wax-sealed; no spark risk | Fragile, heavy | Small quantities, buried storage |
| **Copper canister** | Airtight, non-sparking, durable | Expensive to make; copper corrodes with acidic powder residues | Best all-around container |
| **Brass flask** | Airtight, non-sparking, portable | Small capacity only | Field/hunting powder horn replacement |
| **Glass jar** (amber) | Airtight, visible contents | **Breakable = dangerous.** Fragments become shrapnel. | **NOT RECOMMENDED** |
| **Modern steel ammo can** | Airtight, durable | Steel sparks! Must line with wood or copper | Only if properly lined |

## Quantity Management

**The iron rule: divide your stockpile.**

Never keep all powder in one container or one building. If you have 10 kg total:
- 2 kg in the main magazine (for immediate use)
- 2 kg in a secondary magazine (100 m away)
- 2 kg in buried cache #1 (different compass direction)
- 2 kg in buried cache #2
- 2 kg distributed in personal powder horns/flasks

One fire, one lightning strike, one accident should not eliminate your entire powder supply.

## Shelf Life

Properly made and stored black powder lasts **indefinitely**. Unlike smokeless powder (which degrades chemically over 20вҖ“50 years), black powder is mechanically mixed вҖ” there is no chemical degradation pathway.

However, it can be ruined by:
- **Moisture** вҖ” Saltpetre dissolves and re-crystallises, destroying grain structure
- **Vibration** вҖ” Grains break down to dust over years of movement
- **Temperature cycling** вҖ” Condensation from day/night temperature swings

**Rotate your stock.** Use oldest powder first, replace with fresh. Label every container with manufacture date, batch number, grain size, and test results.

## Desiccants

Place a small cloth bag of dried rice, roasted salt, or quicklime (calcium oxide) inside each storage container. The desiccant absorbs moisture before the powder does. Replace every 3вҖ“6 months.

**Quicklime:** Made by heating limestone or seashells to ~900В°C. Extremely effective desiccant but caustic вҖ” do not let it touch powder. Place in a separate cloth compartment.

## Safety Rules for Storage Areas

{% steps() %}
No flame or spark of any kind within 15 m вҖ” This includes smoking, candles, lanterns (unless in a sealed glass enclosure). Use only sealed electric lighting if available (battery LED) or reflected sunlight.;
No ferrous tools in the magazine вҖ” Wooden scoop, copper funnel, brass measuring cups only.;
Ground yourself before entering вҖ” Touch an earthed copper plate outside the door.;
No synthetic clothing вҖ” Cotton or wool only.;
Keep the door closed except during access вҖ” Minimises moisture ingress and unauthorised access.;
Inventory log inside the door вҖ” Record every addition and removal so you always know your stock.;
Check for moisture weekly вҖ” Inspect desiccant, check for condensation on containers.;
No eating, drinking, or food storage вҖ” Food attracts pests; crumbs attract pests that can compromise containers.
{% end %}

## Emergency Response

If you suspect a fire is approaching the magazine:

1. **Evacuate all personnel to at least 200 m**
2. **Do not attempt to remove powder** вҖ” Running with powder during a fire is how you die
3. **If there is time and the fire is distant (> 500 m), flood the magazine** вҖ” Deliberately soak the powder with water. Wet powder is useless but it won't explode. You can dry and re-process it later
4. **Post sentries to warn others** вҖ” The blast will be heard for kilometres
