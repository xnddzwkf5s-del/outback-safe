# Firearm Safety

Black powder firearms have unique safety considerations beyond modern firearms. The open priming pan, the delay between trigger pull and discharge, the corrosive fouling, and the manual loading process all create hazards that modern firearms training does not cover.

## The Four Universal Rules

These apply to ALL firearms, modern or antique:

1. **Treat every firearm as loaded** — Always. Even if you "know" it's empty. Even if you just checked.
2. **Never point at anything you're not willing to destroy** — The muzzle sweeps everything it crosses.
3. **Finger off the trigger until ready to fire** — Particularly important with a set-trigger or light trigger pull.
4. **Know your target and what's behind it** — A lead ball from a .75 cal musket carries lethal energy beyond 1000 m.

## Black Powder Specific Hazards

### Hangfire

A hangfire is a perceptible delay between the priming flash and the main charge ignition. It feels like an eternity — typically 0.5–2.0 seconds, but every millisecond feels like a second when you think the gun has misfired.

**Cause:** Touch hole partially blocked, powder damp, or fouling insulating the main charge from the flash.

### Misfire

The priming pan flashes but the main charge does not ignite, even after waiting:

{% steps() %}
Wait the full 30 seconds — Count them out.;
Keep the gun pointed downrange — Do not turn it toward yourself or others.;
Pick the touch hole — Use a vent pick to clear any obstruction. Push through into the main charge.;
Re-prime the pan — Fresh priming powder.;
If two re-prime attempts fail — The main charge is likely damp or there is a serious obstruction.;
Unload safely — Pull the ball (see Repair page) or use a CO₂ discharger if available.;
Never attempt to fire a third re-prime — Something is fundamentally wrong.
{% end %}

### Squib Load (Weak Charge)

A squib is a partial charge that fires weakly — you hear a "pop" instead of a "bang." The ball may not exit the barrel.

**Signs:**
- Unusually quiet report
- Weak or no recoil
- Ball does not impact target/backstop

### Cook-Off (Premature Ignition During Loading)

The most terrifying black powder accident. An ember in the barrel ignites the new powder charge while the shooter's hand is over the muzzle.

**Prevention is the only cure:**

{% steps() %}
After every shot, swab the bore — A damp patch extinguishes any remaining embers.;
Wait between shots — Rapid fire builds heat. After 5 shots, let the barrel cool for 1–2 minutes.;
Never pour powder directly from a flask or horn — Use a separate measure. A flask ignition will turn the flask into a grenade at your waist.;
Keep your face and body out of the muzzle path — Pour powder from the side, not directly over the muzzle.;
If you smell burning or see smoke from the muzzle during loading — STOP. Step away. Wait.
{% end %}

### Flash-Back (Pan Flash)

The priming pan ignites, but instead of the flash going through the touch hole, it flashes outward — sometimes toward the shooter's face.

**Prevention:**
- Wear safety glasses ALWAYS
- Keep your face away from the lock area (hold the gun with the lock well forward of your face)
- Ensure the touch hole is not excessively enlarged (see Flintlock page)

### Double Charge

Accidentally loading two powder charges (forgetting you've already loaded).

**Prevention:**
- Always mark the ramrod when ball is seated on powder
- A double-charge mark is higher on the rod than a single-charge mark
- If in doubt, pull the ball and start over

**If you suspect a double charge:** Do not fire it. Pull the ball. A double charge may not burst a properly-proofed barrel immediately, but repeated double-charges will eventually cause catastrophic failure.

## Loading Safety Drills

The safe loading sequence, to be practised until automatic:

```
1. Half-cock the lock           → Safety engaged
2. Swab barrel                  → Extinguishes embers
3. Measure powder (separate)    → NEVER from flask
4. Pour powder into barrel      → Face clear of muzzle
5. Tap butt on ground           → Settle powder
6. Place patch over muzzle      → 
7. Place ball on patch          → 
8. Short-start ball             → Seats ball below muzzle
9. Ram ball fully home          → Steady strokes, no bouncing
10. Verify ramrod depth          → Ball seated on powder
11. Prime pan                     → Close frizzen
12. Full cock (when ready to fire)→ READY
```

## Range and Hunting Safety

### Before Firing

- Know what is downrange for at least 1000 m
- Check for people, livestock, structures in line of fire
- Do not fire at water — balls skip across water unpredictably
- Do not fire at hard, flat surfaces — ricochet danger

### During Group Activities

- One person designated as range officer
- Clear verbal commands: "LOADING", "READY", "FIRING", "CLEAR"
- No one forward of the firing line while ANY gun is loaded
- All guns unloaded and actions open during ceasefires

### When Hunting

- Identify your target positively before raising the gun — "Is that definitely a deer/wallaby/etc.?"
- Do not shoot at movement or sound
- Be aware of where other hunters are
- Load only when actively hunting (unload when returning to camp)

## Storage Safety (Home/Base)

When not in use:

- **Store unloaded** — Powder and ball removed
- **Lock at half-cock** — Prevents accidental trigger contact from dropping cock
- **Frizzen open, pan empty** — Visible confirmation of unloaded state
- **Separate from powder supply** — Gun in living area, powder in magazine
- **Out of reach of children and untrained persons** — Locked rack or cabinet

## Emergency Procedures

### Gunshot Wound (Black Powder)

Black powder wounds are particularly nasty — the low velocity means the ball often stays in the body, and powder residue and patch material are driven deep into the wound.

**Immediate:**
1. Control bleeding with direct pressure
2. Immobilise the injured area
3. Evacuate to medical care

See First Aid section (Tier 1) for detailed wound management. Gunshot wounds are surgical emergencies.

### Powder Burns

**Immediate:**
1. Cool the burn with cool (not ice-cold) water for 10 minutes
2. Cover with clean, dry dressing
3. Do not apply grease, butter, or ointments
4. Seek medical evaluation

### Eye Injury (Pan Flash)

**Immediate:**
1. Do not rub the eye
2. Flush with clean water for 15 minutes
3. Cover both eyes (the injured eye will move with the uninjured eye, causing further damage)
4. Seek medical evaluation urgently
