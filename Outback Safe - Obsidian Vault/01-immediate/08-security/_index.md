# Security — Threats & Perimeter

Security in survival means managing threats to physical safety. These threats come from the environment, from animals, and from other people. A secure camp allows the group to rest, recover, and focus on survival tasks without constant vigilance draining everyone's energy.

---

## Threat Hierarchy

1. **Environment** — weather, terrain, fire, flood (kills most consistently)
2. **Disease/Infection** — from wounds, water, insects (kills over days to weeks)
3. **Animals** — predators, venomous species, aggressive herbivores (injures or kills, but rare)
4. **Other Humans** — depending on the scenario, may be rescuers, neutrals, or threats

## The Security Mindset

Security is not paranoia — it is preparation.

- **Be hard to find** (if concealment is desired) OR **be easy to find** (if rescue is desired). In-between is the worst of both worlds.
- **Be hard to surprise** — early warning systems (visual, audible) give you time to react
- **Be hard to approach undetected** — clear sight lines, obstacles on approach paths
- **Have an escape plan** — if the camp is compromised, where does everyone go?

---

## Day vs. Night Security

| Time | Primary threats | Security focus |
|------|----------------|----------------|
| **Day** | Other humans, some predators (snakes active in heat) | Visual watch, scout patrols, approach management |
| **Night** | Predators (crocodiles, big cats in SEA, dingoes in AU), surprise human approach | Sentry rotation, fire perimeter, noise traps |
