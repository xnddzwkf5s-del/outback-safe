# Fire — Warmth, Purification & Signalling

Fire is the single most powerful survival tool. With fire, you can purify water, cook food, signal for rescue, deter predators, illuminate darkness, dry wet clothing, and maintain morale. Without fire, survival in cold or wet conditions becomes exponentially harder.

**If you can carry only one survival skill, make it fire-starting.**

---

## Why Fire Matters

| Function | Why it saves your life |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Warmth** | Prevents hypothermia; a fire can raise the microclimate temperature by 15–25°C |
| **Water purification** | Boiling kills all pathogens — the gold standard |
| **Cooking** | Breaks down tough foods, kills parasites, increases calorie absorption |
| **Signalling** | Smoke by day, flame by night, three fires in a triangle = international distress |
| **Predator deterrent** | Most animals avoid fire and smoke |
| **Insect repellent** | Smoke drives away mosquitoes and biting flies |
| **Tool making** | Hardening wooden spear points, straightening arrow shafts |
| **Light** | Extends your productive hours into darkness |
| **Morale** | A campfire is psychologically grounding — it is hope you can see and feel |

---

## The Fire Triangle

```
            HEAT
            /\
           /  \
          /    \
         / FIRE \
        /        \
       /__________\
    FUEL          OXYGEN
```

Fire requires three things. Remove any one and the fire dies:

- **Heat** — ignition source (spark, friction, focused sunlight)
- **Fuel** — material that burns (tinder, kindling, fuel wood)
- **Oxygen** — air flow (a smothered fire dies; a well-ventilated fire thrives)

Every fire-starting technique is about uniting these three elements. Every fire failure is about one being insufficient.

---

## Fire Safety

**Before lighting any fire:**

1. Clear a 2 m radius around the fire site — remove all burnable material down to bare earth
2. Ring the fire with rocks (not river rocks — they can explode from trapped water heating)
3. Have water, sand, or a shovel ready to extinguish
4. Do not build fires under overhanging branches — fire climbs
5. In high wind, do not light a fire — sparks travel hundreds of metres
6. Check local conditions: during Australian bushfire season (October–March in the south, May–October in the north), fire risk may be extreme

**Extinguishing:**

{% steps() %}
Douse with water — not just a sprinkle; pour until the hissing stops; Stir the ashes and coals with a stick; Douse again; Hold the back of your hand 5 cm above the ashes — if you feel heat, it is still burning. Repeat.; When completely cold to the touch, the fire is out
{% end %}
