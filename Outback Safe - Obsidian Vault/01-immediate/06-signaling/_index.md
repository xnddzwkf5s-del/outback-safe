# Signaling — Rescue & Communication

Signaling is the bridge between survival and rescue. You can have water, shelter, and fire, but if no one knows you are there, you are surviving indefinitely — not escaping. Effective signaling is not luck; it is a deliberate system.

---

## The Rescue vs. Self-Rescue Decision

| Factor | Favour staying and signaling | Favour self-rescue (moving) |
|--------|----------------------------|---------------------------|
| **You left a trip plan** | Someone is looking for you. Stay. | |
| **You are near a crash/wreck site** | Searchers look for wreckage. Stay near it. | |
| **You are in open terrain** | Visible from air. Signal. | |
| **You have known direction to safety** | | If you know exactly where a road/town is and it is reachable. |
| **No one knows you are missing** | | If no search will be launched, you must self-rescue. |
| **You are in dense canopy** | Air search unlikely to see you. Consider moving to open area. | |
| **You have critical injuries** | Stay. Moving worsens injuries. | |

**Default rule: stay put.** Searchers find stationary survivors much faster than moving ones. Only self-rescue when no search is coming and you have a clear, achievable route to safety.

## The Universal Distress Signal

**Three of anything = distress.**

- Three fires in a triangle
- Three whistle blasts
- Three gunshots
- Three flashes of light
- Three of any signal, repeated at regular intervals

This pattern is recognised internationally by military, search and rescue, aviation, and maritime services.

---

## Signaling Priority — Day vs. Night

| Time | Primary signal | Why |
|------|---------------|-----|
| **Day** | Smoke, ground signals, signal mirror | Smoke visible for kilometres; mirror flash visible for 30+ km |
| **Night** | Fire/flame, light flashes | Flame visible for 10+ km; light flashes further |
| **Dawn/Dusk** | Both | Transition times when searchers are most active |

**Signal continuously when you hear or see potential rescuers.** A signal that is only active "sometimes" is not a signal — it is background.

---

## Where to Signal

- **High ground** — ridge tops, hilltops, clearings on slopes. Visible from all directions.
- **Open areas** — clearings, sandbars, riverbeds (dry), beaches. Avoid canopy cover.
- **Near your shelter** but not AT your shelter — signal area 50–100 m from camp. This keeps your signal visible while your camp stays concealed (if concealment matters) and prevents signal fires from threatening your shelter.

## 🇦🇺 Australian Signaling Context

> **Australia Note:** This page has been adapted for Australian conditions.

### Australian Search & Rescue Context

Australia has one of the most challenging search and rescue environments on Earth — vast distances, sparse population, and extreme conditions.

| Service | Role | Contact |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Royal Flying Doctor Service (RFDS)** | Medical evacuation from remote areas | Via emergency beacon or UHF/VHF radio relay |
| **Australian Maritime Safety Authority (AMSA)** | Coordinates all Australian SAR (land and sea) — manages EPIRB/PLB response | 1800 641 792 (SAR desk) |
| **Police Air Wing / SES** | State-based aerial search | Via 000 or emergency beacon |
| **Volunteer SAR groups** | Ground search teams (SES, Bush Search and Rescue) | State-specific |

**Aerial spotting is the primary search method in Australia.** Ground searches across vast outback areas are impractical — helicopters and fixed-wing aircraft are the primary tools. Your signal must be visible from the air.

### Ground Signals for Aerial Spotting

| Signal type | Minimum size | Best material in AU |
|-------------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Letters (SOS, HELP)** | 3 m tall, 1.5 m wide | White rocks on red outback dirt (maximum contrast) |
| **Contrast signals** | 3 m+ | Dark branches on pale sand / green branches on red dirt / bright fabric on bare ground |
| **Fire scars** | 5 m+ surface | Burned area on spinifex plains — visible from aircraft as a dark patch |
| **Person standing** | Full height | Stand in the open, wave arms, contrast with background |
| **Fabric** | 2 m × 1 m minimum | Orange/red shirt, tarp, tent fly laid out flat |
