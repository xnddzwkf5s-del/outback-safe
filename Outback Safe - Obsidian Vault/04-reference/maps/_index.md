# Maps — Australia & Outback

Navigating the Australian outback is unlike any other environment in the world. Distances between fuel stops can exceed 1,000 km, tracks range from graded gravel to unmarked sand dune crossings, and the penalty for getting lost is measured in days, not hours.

This section provides detailed route references for every major outback track in Australia, including distance tables, fuel stop locations, permit requirements, seasonal access windows, and specific hazards. Use these pages together with physical topographic maps and the [Outback 4WD Survival](01-immediate/09-outback/) section for safe trip planning.
