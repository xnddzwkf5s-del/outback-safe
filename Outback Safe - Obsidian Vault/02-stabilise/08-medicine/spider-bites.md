# Spider Bites

Australia has some of the world's most venomous spiders, but deaths are extremely rare thanks to effective antivenom and public awareness. Two spiders have caused recorded deaths in Australia: the Sydney Funnel-web and the Redback. No spider bite deaths have occurred since the funnel-web antivenom was developed in 1979.

**Key principle:** most spider bites are harmless — but the few dangerous ones need specific, immediate treatment. The wrong first aid (especially with Redback bites) can make things significantly worse.

---

## 1. Sydney Funnel-Web Spider (Atrax robustus)

![Funnel-Web Spider](images/spiders/funnel-web.jpg)
*Sydney Funnel-Web Spider (Atrax robustus). Image: Wikimedia Commons (CC BY-SA).*

- **Appearance:** Large black spider (1-5cm body), shiny carapace, aggressive defensive stance (rears up with front legs, fangs visible)
- **Habitat:** NSW — Sydney region, Central Coast, Blue Mountains, Newcastle. Moist sheltered areas — under rocks, logs, in shoes left outside, garden beds, backyard pools
- **Venom:** Delta-atracotoxin — neurotoxic. Particularly dangerous to humans and other primates (other mammals are largely unaffected)
- **Symptoms:** Severe pain at bite site (immediate), tingling around mouth and tongue, facial muscle spasms, profuse salivation, sweating, piloerection (goosebumps — a telltale sign), confusion, severe hypertension, pulmonary edema, coma
- **Onset:** Rapid — symptoms can begin within 15 minutes. Death has occurred in under 2 hours in untreated cases
- **Treatment:** PRESSURE IMMOBILISATION BANDAGE (same technique as snake bite — one of the few non-snake uses of PIB)
- **Antivenom:** Available and highly effective. No deaths since 1981 (antivenom introduced 1979)
- **First aid:** Immobilise limb, keep victim still, apply splint, call 000 immediately

{% warning() %}
**Funnel-web bites are a medical emergency.** Apply PIB immediately and call 000. If in a remote area, activate a PLB. Symptoms can progress to life-threatening within 30 minutes.
{% end %}

## 2. Other Funnel-Web Species

Several other funnel-web species can cause severe envenomation. All require the same first aid as Sydney Funnel-web.

| Species | Distribution | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| **Northern Tree Funnel-web** (Hadronyche formidabilis) | QLD/NSW border region | Very dangerous — found in tree stumps, logs |
| **Blue Mountains Funnel-web** | Blue Mountains NSW | Also dangerous, similar venom |
| **Southern Funnel-web** (Hadronyche cerberea) | Victoria, NSW south coast | Less toxic but still requires PIB |
| **Port Macquarie Funnel-web** (Hadronyche macquariensis) | Mid-north coast NSW | Potentially dangerous |

**Treatment:** Same as Sydney Funnel-web — apply PIB, immobilise, call 000.

## 3. Redback Spider (Latrodectus hasselti)

![Redback Spider](images/spiders/redback.jpg)
*Female Redback Spider (Latrodectus hasselti). The distinctive red dorsal stripe is a key identifying feature. Image: Wikimedia Commons (CC BY-SA).*

- **Appearance:** Small black spider with distinctive red/orange dorsal stripe. Female up to 1cm body, male much smaller and not medically dangerous
- **Habitat:** ALL Australian states — urban, rural, outback. Under toilet seats, outdoor furniture, rubbish piles, car door handles, under vehicle seats, letterboxes, children's toys left outside
- **Venom:** Latrotoxin — presynaptic neurotoxin causing massive neurotransmitter release. Painful but rarely life-threatening
- **Symptoms:** Intense local pain (minutes after bite), spreading proximally (moving up the limb), **sweating at bite site (localised diaphoresis — a diagnostic sign unique to Redback bites)**, muscle pain and spasms, abdominal rigidity (can mimic acute abdomen and lead to unnecessary surgery), headache, nausea, hypertension
- **Onset:** Pain builds over 1-3 hours. Can persist for days without antivenom
- **Treatment:** ICE PACK for pain relief. **DO NOT use PIB** (contraindicated — may worsen pain significantly)
- **Antivenom:** Redback antivenom available at all Australian hospitals. Effective for severe envenomation. Deaths extremely rare — none since 1955

{% warning() %}
**Redback treatment is the opposite of funnel-web.** Never apply pressure immobilisation to a Redback bite. Use ice packs. Pressure will make the pain dramatically worse.
{% end %}

## 4. Mouse Spider (Missulena species)

- **Appearance:** Stocky, dark spider, often confused with funnel-web. Large fangs. Males have distinctive red head/chelicerae (jaw parts)
- **Habitat:** Widespread across Australia. Burrows in ground — often encountered during gardening or digging
- **Venom:** Similar neurotoxins to funnel-web. Same antivenom works for both
- **Envenomation rate:** LOW — frequently gives dry bites or bites without injecting significant venom
- **Treatment:** Apply PIB if in funnel-web habitat and not certain of species identification. Safe to treat as funnel-web until proven otherwise
- **Hospital:** Yes — for observation. Most cases do not require antivenom

---

## 5. White-Tailed Spider (Lampona species)

- **Appearance:** Dark, cigar-shaped body with distinctive white spot at tail tip. 1-2cm body length. Long, slender legs
- **Habitat:** Southern and eastern Australia. Often found indoors — bedding, clothing, curtains, bathrooms. Hunts other spiders at night
- **Venom:** Mild necrotoxin. The myth of flesh-eating wounds has been thoroughly debunked in multiple clinical studies
- **Symptoms:** Local pain, mild swelling, irritation. Ulcer formation is RARE and usually due to secondary infection or other underlying causes
- **Treatment:** Clean wound with antiseptic. Ice for pain. Monitor for signs of infection. No antivenom needed — none exists
- **Key message:** NOT medically dangerous despite its fearsome reputation. Do not panic if bitten

---

## 6. When to Seek Help

| Spider | Treatment | Hospital? |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Funnel-web | PIB, immobilise | IMMEDIATELY — call 000 |
| Redback | Ice pack, pain relief | Yes if severe pain or systemic symptoms |
| Mouse spider | PIB (treat as funnel-web) | Yes — for observation |
| White-tailed | Clean wound, ice | Only if infection develops |
| All others | Wash, ice pack | Not needed unless allergic reaction |
