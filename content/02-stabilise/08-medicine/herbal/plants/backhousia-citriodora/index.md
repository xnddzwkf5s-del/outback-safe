# Backhousia citriodora

![Lemon Myrtle](/images/plants/backhousia-citriodora.jpg)
*Backhousia citriodora — Lemon myrtle. Potent antimicrobial. Image: Wikimedia Commons (CC BY-SA).*

## Common Names
- English: Lemon myrtle, lemon-scented myrtle, sweet verbena tree
- Chinese: 柠檬香桃木 (níngméng xiāng táo mù)
- Aboriginal: Various names in language groups of coastal Queensland

## Identification
- **Habitat:** Subtropical rainforest of coastal Queensland, from Mackay to Brisbane. Grows as an understory tree in moist, sheltered gullies. Requires high rainfall and humidity.
- **Leaves:** The defining feature. Glossy green, elliptical, 5–12 cm long, opposite, with undulating (wavy) margins. Intensely lemon-scented when crushed — among the highest natural citral concentrations of any plant (90–98% of essential oil). Young leaves are soft and paler green.
- **Flowers:** Small (5–8 mm), white to cream, numerous stamens, clustered in dense heads at branch tips. Appear in summer to autumn. Sweetly fragrant.
- **Bark:** Grey-brown, somewhat flaky in older trees, smooth on younger branches. Not the medicinal part.
- **Roots:** Not medicinally used.
- **Look-alikes:** Other Backhousia species (e.g., *B. myrtifolia*, grey myrtle) have leaves that may look similar but lack the intense lemon fragrance — the scent is the definitive ID. Do not confuse with citrus trees — citrus leaves have oil glands visible when held to light and petioles are often winged.

## Distribution
Native to coastal Queensland rainforests from Mackay to Brisbane. Cultivated commercially in northern NSW and Queensland. Not naturally occurring in China or SEA, but could potentially be grown in similar subtropical climates.

## Medicinal Uses
- **Primary:** Topical antimicrobial — among the most powerful natural antiseptics. The high citral content provides broad-spectrum antibacterial and antifungal activity. Effective against *Staphylococcus aureus*, *E. coli*, *Candida albicans*, and many other pathogens. Use as wound wash, skin infection treatment, and surface disinfectant.
- **Secondary:** Respiratory decongestant and antiviral. Steam inhalations for sinusitis, colds, and flu. Insect repellent (citral repels mosquitoes). Flavour for food and medicine (strong lemon taste).
- **Traditional:** Aboriginal peoples used crushed leaves as an insect repellent, and leaf infusions for headaches, colds, and general illness. The leaves were also used in cooking for flavour.

## Preparation
- **Infusion:** 1/2–1 teaspoon dried leaves (it's very strong) per 250 mL boiling water, steep covered 5–8 minutes. Drink 1–2 cups daily for cold/flu. Use cooled infusion as wound wash or mouth rinse (excellent for oral thrush).
- **Poultice:** Crush fresh leaves, apply to minor wounds or fungal skin infections. Cover, change every 2–3 hours.
- **Tincture:** Fresh leaves in 60% alcohol, steep 3–4 weeks. Dose: 1–2 mL in water, 2–3× daily. For topical: dab on cold sores, skin infections with cotton.
- **Steam Inhalation:** Small handful of fresh leaves (or 3–5 drops oil) in hot water. Inhale 5–10 minutes. Very effective for sinus congestion.
- **Surface Disinfectant:** Strong decoction (2 tablespoons leaves per 250 mL) cooled — use to wipe surfaces, clean wounds.

## Safety
- **Contraindications:** Rare but possible skin sensitivity to concentrated citral. Avoid contact with eyes. Do not use undiluted essential oil on skin. Avoid in pregnancy (limited safety data for medicinal doses).
- **Side effects:** Contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals — patch test before regular use. Mucous membrane irritation if infusion is too strong.
- **Interactions:** No significant known interactions.
- **Toxicity:** Generally recognised as safe in food/tea quantities. Essential oil should not be ingested in large amounts — citral is a strong mucosal irritant.

## Cultivation
- **Propagation:** Fresh seed (sow immediately, viability drops quickly) or semi-hardwood cuttings with hormone treatment. Cuttings have moderate success rate. Can also propagate by layering.
- **Growing conditions:** Subtropical to warm temperate. Prefers partial shade (understory tree), rich moist soil, high humidity. Protect from frost — sensitive to temperatures below -2°C. Will grow in full sun if well-watered. Slow to moderate growth rate.
- **Harvest:** Leaves harvested year-round. Highest oil content in young, fully expanded leaves. Prune regularly to encourage bushy growth and higher leaf yield. Dry in shade at low temperature to preserve volatile oils.
