# Gunpowder Safety

If you read nothing else in the Weapons section, read this page. Gunpowder manufacture has killed experienced professionals who became complacent. **Complacency kills.**

## The Hazards

Black powder presents four distinct hazards:

| Hazard | Mechanism | Prevention |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Dust explosion** | Fine powder suspended in air ignites | Never let powder dust accumulate; work in open air |
| **Impact ignition** | Friction or impact from metal tools | Use only wooden/copper/brass tools; no iron or steel |
| **Static discharge** | Spark ignites powder | Ground yourself; no synthetic clothing; control humidity |
| **Thermal runaway** | Heat buildup in stored powder | Store small quantities; separate containers; cool location |

## Protective Equipment

{% materials() %}
Leather or cotton work apron — no synthetics, which generate static; Safety goggles or face shield — full coverage, side protection; Leather gloves — no metal rivets or snaps; Cotton or wool clothing — no polyester, nylon, fleece; Leather boots — no steel toe caps; Hair tied back — or wear a cotton cap; No jewellery — rings, watches, metal bracelets all removed
{% end %}

## Workspace Requirements

{% steps() %}
**Work outdoors or in a dedicated open-air structure** — Never manufacture gunpowder in a living space, kitchen, or enclosed workshop. The ideal workspace is a three-walled shelter with open front, roof, and dirt floor.;
**Have a clear escape path** — You should be able to exit in under 3 seconds from any position. Keep the path clear at all times.;
**Separate workstations by at least 5 metres** — Grinding station, mixing station, pressing station, and drying station should each be isolated so a failure at one does not chain to others.;
**Use wooden or earthen work surfaces** — No metal tables, no concrete (which can spark if struck with metal). Heavy hardwood slab or compacted earth.;
**Install a grounding system** — Drive a copper rod into damp earth, connect yourself with a conductive strap. At minimum, touch a grounded object frequently.;
**Keep a fire bucket within reach** — Filled with dry sand, not water. Water spreads burning powder.;
**Post a lookout** — If working with others, one person watches for hazards. No solo work with mixed powder.
{% end %}

## Quantity Limits

**Never process more than 100 g of mixed powder at a time** during manufacturing. Even this amount can cause severe injury. Scale up only if you have successfully completed dozens of small batches with no incidents, and only if you have a purpose-built magazine for storage.

For storage limits, see the Storage page.

## The Seven Rules

1. **No ignition sources anywhere near the workspace** — No smoking, no flames, no sparking tools
2. **No metal tools that can spark** — Iron and steel are prohibited. Use wood, brass, copper, or bone
3. **Work damp whenever possible** — Wet incorporation is safer than dry mixing
4. **Never grind mixed powder** — Grind each ingredient separately, mix only when damp
5. **One batch at a time** — Complete and store each batch before starting the next
6. **Never leave powder unattended** — Mixed powder should be in secure storage or under active supervision
7. **If you feel any heat, stop immediately** — Friction warming during mixing is your last warning

## What To Do If It Catches Fire

If powder ignites during handling:

1. **Do not try to extinguish with water** — It spreads the burning powder
2. **Smother with dry sand from the fire bucket**
3. **If on your clothing, drop and roll** — Standard stop-drop-roll procedure
4. **If a large quantity ignites, RUN** — Do not attempt to fight it. Get clear immediately
5. **Treat burns with cool water** — See First Aid section (Tier 1)

## Training Protocol

Before touching any ingredient, train every team member:

{% steps() %}
Read this entire page aloud to the group — Everyone acknowledges they understand.;
Demonstrate workspace setup — Identify grounding, escape path, fire bucket, workstations.;
Walk through a safety drill — Simulate: "There's a fire at the mixing station." Everyone practices evacuation.;
Supervised first batch — New person works under direct supervision of most experienced team member.;
Solo sign-off — Only after 5 supervised batches with zero safety violations.
{% end %}
