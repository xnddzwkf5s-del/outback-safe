# Mixing and Incorporating Gunpowder

Mixing is the most dangerous step in gunpowder manufacture. A dust explosion at this stage can be fatal. **Read the Safety page first.** Work small, work damp, work outdoors.

## The Standard Ratio

```
75% Potassium Nitrate (saltpetre)
15% Charcoal
10% Sulfur
```

All measurements by weight. Use a balance scale, not volume вҖ” densities vary dramatically.

## Mixing Methods (Ranked by Safety)

### Method 1: Wet Incorporation (Safest, Recommended)

This is the historical method used in all proper powder mills from the 15th century onwards. The ingredients are ground together while wet, forming a paste that is later dried and granulated.

{% steps() %}
**Weigh ingredients precisely** вҖ” For a 100 g batch: 75 g saltpetre, 15 g charcoal, 10 g sulfur. Use a balance scale.;
**SIEVE each dry ingredient through fine cloth** вҖ” Removing any lumps before they meet.;
**Mix charcoal and sulfur first (dry)** вҖ” Gently tumble in a wooden bowl until uniform grey. These two are not explosive together and can be handled with less caution.;
**Add distilled water to the charcoal-sulfur mix** вҖ” Enough to make a thin paste (approximately 30вҖ“40 mL per 100 g of final powder). Stir with a wooden spatula.;
**Add the saltpetre gradually** вҖ” While stirring continuously with a wooden paddle. The saltpetre dissolves partially in the water, coating every particle of charcoal and sulfur.;
**Work the paste for 15вҖ“20 minutes** вҖ” Kneading, folding, and pressing with wooden tools. This is "incorporation" вҖ” ensuring every charcoal and sulfur particle contacts saltpetre. The paste should be uniform dark grey-brown, with no streaks.;
**Form into a flat cake about 1 cm thick** вҖ” On a wooden board or cloth.;
**Allow to air-dry until leather-hard** вҖ” Approximately 2вҖ“4 hours in warm, shaded conditions. Do not force-dry with heat at this stage.;
**SAFETY CHECKPOINT**: Is the paste cool to touch? No warm spots? Continue.;
**Granulate the leather-hard cake** вҖ” Break into chunks and push through a sieve (see grain sizes below).;
**Spread granules on cloth in a single layer** вҖ” Dry completely in warm shade (24вҖ“48 hours). Stir gently every few hours.
{% end %}

### Method 3: Mortar and Pestle (Small Scale Only)

For batches under 50 g:

{% steps() %}
Dampen the charcoal-sulfur mix with distilled water вҖ” As in wet incorporation above.;
Add saltpetre and grind GENTLY вҖ” Use a wooden or bronze mortar and pestle. Never iron or steel.;
Work for 5вҖ“10 minutes вҖ” Until the mixture is uniform and pasty.;
Proceed to drying and granulation as in Method 1.
{% end %}

## Grain Sizes

The grain size determines burn rate. Larger grains burn more slowly and are used for larger charges.

| Grain Name | Sieve Size | Typical Use | Burn Speed |
|------------|-----------|-------------|------------|
| **Meal** | < 0.5 mm (passes fine screen) | Priming pans, flash powder | Very fast |
| **FFFFg (4F)** | 0.5вҖ“0.8 mm | Priming flintlocks, small pistols | Fast |
| **FFFG (3F)** | 0.8вҖ“1.2 mm | Pistols, small-bore rifles | Medium-fast |
| **FFG (2F)** | 1.2вҖ“1.7 mm | Muskets, rifles, shotguns | Medium |
| **FG (1F)** | 1.7вҖ“2.4 mm | Large muskets, blunderbusses | Medium-slow |
| **Cannon** | 2.4вҖ“4.0 mm | Cannons, large blasting charges | Slow |
| **Large cannon** | 4.0вҖ“10 mm | Siege cannons, mining | Very slow |

Wooden frames, stretched brass or copper wire mesh вҖ” never iron or steel wire.

### Granulation Procedure

{% steps() %}
Ensure the paste is leather-hard вҖ” Not wet enough to stick together, not dry enough to shatter to dust.;
Push through the coarsest desired sieve вҖ” Rotate a wooden scraper over the mesh. Granules fall through as irregular grains.;
Collect what passes through each sieve вҖ” What stays on top of a screen is that grain size.;
Re-grind oversized grains вҖ” Return to paste stage with a little water.;
Dust (what passes all sieves) can be re-pressed or used as priming powder вҖ” But meal powder burns too fast for use as a main charge in anything larger than a priming pan.

**Drying:** Spread granules on cloth in single layer. Dry for 24вҖ“48 hours in warm (25вҖ“35В°C), shaded, ventilated area. Turn gently every 4вҖ“6 hours.
{% end %}

## Quality Indicators After Mixing

| Observation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| Uniform dark grey colour | Good incorporation |
| Light or dark streaks | Poor mixing вҖ” re-incorporate |
| White crystals visible | Undissolved saltpetre вҖ” grind finer before mixing |
| Yellow spots | Unmixed sulfur вҖ” grind finer |
| Crumbly, won't hold grain shape | Too dry during granulation вҖ” mist lightly with water |
| Sticky, clumps into balls | Too wet вҖ” let it dry more before sieving |

## Polishing (Optional)

Commercial powder was often "polished" by tumbling the dried grains in a rotating wooden barrel for several hours. This rounds the sharp edges, reducing dust and making the grains more resistant to breaking during transport.

For survival use, polishing is optional. It improves consistency but adds a processing step with its own safety considerations (dust generation in the tumbler).
