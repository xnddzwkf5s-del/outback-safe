# Towers

A tower is height turned into tactical advantage. It extends your line of sight, extends your effective weapons range, and provides a protected fighting position that overlooks the approaches and the walls themselves.

## Why Towers?

1. **Extended visibility** вҖ” From a 10 m tower, your horizon is approximately 11 km (vs. 5 km at ground level). You see threats earlier.
2. **Gravity assist** вҖ” Arrows, bolts, and thrown objects gain energy when launched downward
3. **Enfilading fire** вҖ” Towers projecting from walls allow defenders to shoot along the face of adjacent wall sections, catching attackers between two lines of fire
4. **Overlook your own walls** вҖ” If attackers reach the top of your wall, the tower provides an elevated position to shoot down at them
5. **Communication** вҖ” Visual signals (flags, smoke, mirrors) from a tower are visible for kilometres
6. **Safe storage** вҖ” The upper levels of a tower are the most secure place in your compound

**Corner towers:** At every corner of the perimeter. These see two faces of the wall and provide converging fire.

**Interval towers:** Along straight wall sections, spaced so that arrows from one tower reach the base of the next tower. Maximum spacing: 40 m for bows, 60 m for crossbows, 80 m for firearms.

**Gate towers:** Flanking the main gate. Two towers, one on each side, connected by a wall walk above the gate. This creates the barbican/gatehouse with defenders on three sides of the gate passage.

### Freestanding Towers (Keep/Dungeon)

A large central tower (keep) serves as:
- Last defensive position if outer walls are breached
- Secure storage for critical supplies (food, water, weapons, powder)
- Command post with best visibility
- Living quarters for key personnel

### Materials

**Timber tower (fastest, simplest):**

{% steps() %}
Set four main corner posts вҖ” Heavy logs, 25вҖ“30 cm diameter, set 1вҖ“1.5 m into the ground. Posts extend to the full tower height.;
Frame the floors вҖ” Horizontal beams at each floor level, mortise-and-tenon or lashed joints.;
Install floor joists вҖ” Spanning between the beams, covered with planks.;
Frame the walls вҖ” Lighter timbers between the corner posts, leaving arrow loop gaps.;
Install the roof вҖ” Waterproof (bark, shingles, thatch, or salvaged corrugated iron).;
Build the fighting platform вҖ” The uppermost level is open, with a timber parapet (breastwork) for defenders to shoot over.;
Build the stairs/ladder вҖ” Internal or external. Ladder between floors conserves interior space.
{% end %}

**Earth-and-timber tower (motte):**

A conical mound of earth (motte) with a timber tower on top. This is the classic Norman motte-and-bailey design.

{% steps() %}
Build the earth mound вҖ” Pile earth in layers, compacting each layer. The mound should be 3вҖ“5 m high, 15вҖ“20 m diameter at base, 5вҖ“8 m diameter at top. Slope the sides at 45В°.;
Face the mound вҖ” Turf or stone to prevent erosion.;
Build the timber tower on top вҖ” As above, but smaller footprint.;
Build a bridge or staircase to the top вҖ” A steep timber stair or ladder.;
Surround the base with a ditch вҖ” The ditch and mound together create a serious obstacle.
{% end %}

**Stone tower (permanent, labour-intensive):**

See the Construction section (Tier 3) for stone masonry techniques, but key points:

- Walls batter (taper inward) as they rise вҖ” a 2 m base wall may be 1.2 m at the top
- Corner quoins (large, well-dressed stones at corners) are essential вҖ” they tie the walls together
- Vaulted ceilings (barrel vaults) between floors вҖ” these are fireproof and support the weight above
- Spiral staircases in the wall thickness conserve interior space

- **Merlon:** The solid upright section. Should be wide enough to completely conceal a person (minimum 60 cm).
- **Embrasure (crenel):** The gap. Wide enough to shoot through (30вҖ“50 cm).
- **Height:** Merlons should be 1.5вҖ“2.0 m tall. Embrasures should be deep enough (1.0вҖ“1.5 m from floor) that a defender has cover while shooting.

The exterior opening is narrow (5вҖ“8 cm) to minimise exposure. The interior opening flares wide (60вҖ“80 cm) to allow the archer to traverse (aim side to side) and to admit light.

Vertical slits allow shooting at various elevations. A horizontal cross-slit (cruciform) allows wider traverse at the cost of larger external opening.

## Tower Roof

A tower roof serves two purposes: weather protection and a fighting platform.

### Flat Roof with Parapet

The most useful design вҖ” the roof is a fighting platform:

{% steps() %}
Install heavy roof beams вҖ” Spanning the tower width, projecting slightly beyond the walls.;
Cover with planks вҖ” Tightly fitted.;
Waterproof вҖ” Multiple layers of birch bark, tarred canvas, or modern roofing material if available. Pitch or tar if you can produce it.;
Cover with a layer of gravel or thin stone slabs вҖ” Fireproofing. A flaming arrow landing on bare timber roof will set the tower alight.;
Build the parapet (crenellations) вҖ” Around the entire perimeter.;
Install drain spouts вҖ” Projecting stone or timber spouts (gargoyles) to shed rainwater clear of the walls.
{% end %}

## Tower Armament

What to store in a tower:

| Item | Location | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Arrows/bolts** | Upper level | Ready for immediate use from fighting platform |
| **Rocks** (fist-sized to head-sized) | Upper level | Dropped from height = devastating. Stack neatly. |
| **Spears** | Upper and middle levels | For thrusting down at climbers |
| **Boiling water/cauldron** | Upper level | Fire pit for heating water to pour on attackers |
| **Firearms and powder** | Middle level (secure, dry) | NOT on the open fighting platform (spark risk from fire pit) |
| **Food and water** | Ground level (cool, accessible) | Enough for the tower garrison for 1 week minimum |
| **Spare weapons** | Middle level | Bows, bowstrings, arrows, spare flints |

## Communication Between Towers

Towers should be able to communicate:

- **Voice** вҖ” Practical between towers up to 30 m apart (yelling). Beyond that, use signals.
- **Horn/whistle** вҖ” Pre-arranged signals (one blast = all clear, two = alert, three = attack on wall section)
- **Flag signals** вҖ” During daylight. Coloured flags or simple position codes.
- **Signal fire / smoke** вҖ” At night (fire) or day (smoke by adding green vegetation). Primary tower ignites, others relay.
- **Bell** вҖ” A bell in the main tower can be heard throughout the compound.

Establish a simple signal code and ensure every tower occupant knows it.

## Tower Defense

If attackers scale a wall and reach a tower:

- **The tower base door should open inward** (easy to barricade from inside)
- **Spiral staircases favour right-handed defenders** вҖ” Defenders retreating upward have their sword arm free; attackers climbing have their sword arm against the central pillar (assuming clockwise spiral)
- **Storage of projectiles at every level** вҖ” So defenders retreating upward are not unarmed
- **The roof is the last stand** вҖ” Defenders at the top can hold off attackers on the stairs (one-at-a-time bottleneck)
