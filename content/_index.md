Your Australian remote travel reference. Whether you're crossing the Simpson Desert, tackling the Canning Stock Route, or exploring Cape York — this guide has what you need when you're hours or days from help.

**No internet required.** Download the full site, put it on a USB stick or your phone, and you have access to everything even with zero mobile reception.

## What's Inside

### 🚙 Outback 4WD Survival
Route planning, vehicle preparation, breakdown protocol, heat survival, bush mechanics, vehicle recovery, water management, navigation without GPS. **Everything you need before and during remote outback travel.**

### 🏥 Medicine & First Aid
Comprehensive snake bite reference (10 Australian species), remote area first aid (wound closure, fractures, burns, dental, childbirth), outback bush medicine (what plants are useful and what will kill you), dehydration management.

### 🗺️ Maps & Track Guides
Detailed route information for all major outback tracks — Simpson Desert, Cape York, Canning Stock Route, Gibb River Road, Anne Beadell Highway, Binns Track — plus Birdsville Track, Oodnadatta Track, Tanami Track, and more. Every page includes free Geoscience Australia topo map download links, offline app recommendations, and track-specific hazards.

### 📻 Communications
146 amateur radio repeaters across all 8 Australian states and territories — frequencies, CTCSS tones, call signs. UHF CB channels, satellite phone guide, PLB activation, WICEN emergency communications, APRS tracking setup.

### 🏥 Remote First Aid
Wound closure, splinting, dental emergencies, fractures, burns, head injury, childbirth in remote areas. Snake bite, spider bite, marine stingers, tick bites, anaphylaxis.

### 🌿 Bush Medicine
26 medicinal plants found in arid and tropical outback Australia, with photos, preparation methods, and deadly look-alike warnings.

## Designed for Zero Connectivity

- **Works offline** — all content is static HTML. No server, no JavaScript required.
- **Fits on a USB stick** — entire site under 5MB.
- **Print-ready** — every page has print styles for hard copy.
- **Battery-friendly** — no animations, minimal resources.
- **Open source** — CC BY-SA 4.0. Free to use, share, and modify.

## How to Use

1. **Download the site** before your trip — save the entire `/site/` folder to your device
2. **Browse the sections relevant to your route**
3. **Print the pages for your specific track** and emergency first aid
4. **Carry hard copies** — paper doesn't run out of battery

### 📱 Save on iPhone/iPad for Offline Use

**Website (read in Safari):** Open this website → tap Share → **Add to Home Screen**. Browse a few pages on first visit — the service worker caches everything for permanent offline access, even in airplane mode.

**EPUB (read in Books):** [📖 Download EPUB](Outback Safe.epub) — then:

1. Download the EPUB — it saves to the **Files** app
2. Install the free **Books** app from the App Store (orange icon)
3. In Files, locate `Outback Safe.epub` → long-press → **Share** → **Books**
4. Opens in Books with adjustable font, dark mode, and all 60+ photos
