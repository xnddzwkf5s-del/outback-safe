# Pre-Trip Checklist

Use this checklist before every outback trip. Print it. Check every item. Your life may depend on it.

{% warning() %}
This checklist complements the detailed guides in this section — it is a summary, not a substitute. Read [Vehicle Breakdown: Stay or Go?](01-immediate/09-outback/vehicle-breakdown), [Extreme Heat Survival](01-immediate/09-outback/heat-survival), and [What to Carry](01-immediate/09-outback/what-to-carry) before departure.
{% end %}

---

## Vehicle Preparation

| Item | Check |
|------|-------|
| Fuel: range calculated for longest leg + 30% reserve | ☐ |
| Spare tyres: 2 minimum (matching size) | ☐ |
| Radiator hoses checked for cracks & leaks | ☐ |
| Coolant topped up; spare coolant carried | ☐ |
| Oil & all fluids topped; spare bottles carried | ☐ |
| Battery terminals clean; spare jump leads | ☐ |
| Recovery gear: snatch strap, rated shackles, shovel, traction boards | ☐ |
| Tyre pressure gauge & compressor (12V) | ☐ |
| Basic tool kit spanners, sockets, screwdrivers, multi-tool | ☐ |
| Duct tape, zip ties, wire, silicone sealant | ☐ |
| Jack & base plate for soft ground | ☐ |
| First-aid-rated fire extinguisher (ABE, in-cab reach) | ☐ |

{% info() %}
Corrugations are the #1 cause of outback vehicle failure. Take it slow — 30–40 km/h on bad corrugations reduces stress on suspension and radiator mounts. Faster is not safer.
{% end %}

{% info() %}
Reduce tyre pressure to 20–25 psi (135–170 kPa) for sand driving, 28 psi (190 kPa) for corrugations. Remember to reinflate when back on sealed roads or you risk tyre overheating at highway speeds.
{% end %}

## Permits & Access

| Item | Check |
|------|-------|
| National Park camping & entry permits (booked online, printed) | ☐ |
| Aboriginal Land permits (Northern Territory, APY Lands, etc.) | ☐ |
| Station access permissions (phone call or email) | ☐ |
| Desert Parks Pass (South Australia — Simpson Desert, Birdsville Track) | ☐ |
| Firewood collection permit (if applicable) | ☐ |
| Fishing licence (if applicable) | ☐ |

{% warning() %}
Entering Aboriginal Land without a permit is an offence under federal and state law. Fines are substantial, and you may be escorted off the land. Apply at least 4 weeks in advance through the relevant land council.
{% end %}

## Communications Setup

| Item | Check |
|------|-------|
| UHF radio fitted & tested (channels 5, 10, 40 saved as presets) | ☐ |
| Satellite phone charged & test call made | ☐ |
| PLB/EPIRB registration confirmed (AMSA website) & battery in date | ☐ |
| Scheduled check-in times agreed with reliable contact | ☐ |
| 24-hour emergency numbers stored offline (paper, not phone) | ☐ |
| RACQ / RAA / NRMA off-road membership active & numbers saved | ☐ |
| Charging cables for all devices (12V + USB) | ☐ |
| Paper maps + compass carried (GPS is backup, not primary) | ☐ |

{% warning() %}
Do not skip the scheduled check-in. It is your only lifeline. If you miss a check-in and cannot be raised within 12 hours, SES or Police will be notified. Define this with your contact before departure: "If you haven't heard from me by 08:00 on [date], call 000 and tell them my planned route and PLB details."
{% end %}

## Water & Food

| Item | Check |
|------|-------|
| Water: 7 L per person per day minimum (drinking + cooking) | ☐ |
| Extra water: additional 7 L per person for breakdown contingencies | ☐ |
| Food: full trip supply + 3 days emergency rations | ☐ |
| Water purification backup (tablets, filter, or boil vessel) | ☐ |
| Electrolyte powder or salts for heat conditions | ☐ |
| Cooking fuel sufficient for extended stay | ☐ |

## Medical Kit

| Item | Check |
|------|-------|
| Snake bite bandages: 4 x 10 cm wide crepe or specialised PIB bandage | ☐ |
| Comprehensive first aid kit (bandages, antiseptic, gloves, tweezers) | ☐ |
| Personal medications: full course + 7 days extra | ☐ |
| EpiPen (if prescribed — check expiry date) | ☐ |
| Antihistamines (non-drowsy) | ☐ |
| Pain relief (paracetamol, ibuprofen) | ☐ |
| Antiseptic (povidone-iodine or chlorhexidine) | ☐ |
| Sunscreen (SPF 50+, broad spectrum) & lip balm | ☐ |
| Insect repellent (DEET or picaridin based) | ☐ |
| Blister treatment kit | ☐ |

## Weather & Conditions

| Item | Check |
|------|-------|
| BOM 7-day forecast checked for entire route | ☐ |
| BOM river height warnings checked (flood risk for crossings) | ☐ |
| Fire danger ratings checked (CFS, CFA, RFS websites) | ☐ |
| Total fire ban rules understood for each state | ☐ |
| Road condition reports checked (Main Roads WA, SA, NT, QLD, NSW) | ☐ |
| Seasonal closures checked (e.g. Simpson Desert closed Dec–Mar) | ☐ |
| Local advice sought (roadhouses, station managers, visitor centres) | ☐ |

{% info() %}
The BOM app and website work well with mobile reception. Download the forecast pages and save them offline before you leave reception. River height warnings are critical in the wet season — a normally dry creek can become an impassable torrent within hours after upstream rain.
{% end %}

---

## Route Card Filed

| Item | Check |
|------|-------|
| Planned route documented with distances and landmarks | ☐ |
| Campsites identified for each night | ☐ |
| Fuel stops confirmed (know distances between fuel availability) | ☐ |
| Expected timeline shared with reliable contact | ☐ |
| PLB registration number & description included in filed route card | ☐ |
| Alternative routes / escape routes noted | ☐ |
| All gateways, locks, and keys confirmed accessible | ☐ |

{% warning() %}
File your route card with **two** people: one person who will be driving the route with you (even if just email), and a separate reliable contact who will stay put. If the driver goes down, the route card is useless. The stationary contact must know: where you're going, when to call 000, and your PLB hex ID.
{% end %}

---

## Final Check

Everything above is checked? Good. Now take a photo of your fully stocked vehicle with date stamp, send it to your route card contact, and hit the road. Drive safe.
