# What to Carry

This checklist assumes a 3-day, 4-person outback 4WD trip. Adjust quantities for your trip duration and group size. Every item on this list has saved lives.

---

## Water — The Critical Priority

| Item | Quantity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Drinking water | 10 L per person per day × (trip days + 2 extra) | For a 3-day trip with 4 people: 10 × 4 × 5 = 200 L minimum |
| Water containers | Multiple 10–20 L jerry cans | Separate cans for each day — if one leaks you don't lose everything |
| Collapsible water bags | 2–4 × 10 L | Backup storage, easy to stow empty |
| Electrolyte powder / salt | 1 packet per person per day | Prevents hyponatraemia (water poisoning). Table salt works. |

**Example calculation — 3 day trip, 4 people:**

Formula: (trip days + 2 emergency days) × people × 10 L per person per day

= 5 days × 4 people × 10 L = 200 L

This is about 10 standard 20 L jerry cans. For longer trips, cache water along the route or pre-arrange resupply drops.

## Communication

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **PLB (Personal Locator Beacon)** or **EPIRB** | **Highest priority purchase.** Triggers AMSA search within hours. Must be registered with AMSA. |
| **UHF radio** | 5 W minimum. 40–50 km range on flat ground. Know the channels. |
| **Satellite phone** | Only if budget allows. Iridium or Globalstar for Australia. |
| **Mobile phone** | Fully charged. Offline maps downloaded. Power bank (20,000 mAh+). |
| **Car charger / inverter** | To charge devices from vehicle. |
| **Paper list of emergency contacts** | RFDS, SES, local police station numbers. |

### UHF Channels to Know

| Channel | Use |
|---------|-----|
| Ch 5 | **EMERGENCY ONLY** (repeater-enabled, legal restriction) — start here in distress |
| Ch 10 | 4WD clubs and convoys |
| Ch 11 | **CALLING channel** — make contact, then move to another channel |
| Ch 18 | Caravan and camper convoys |
| Ch 29 | Pacific Highway (NSW/QLD) |
| Ch 40 | **HIGHWAY** — trucks, road trains, general highway use. Monitor this for help. |
| Ch 35 (Deprecated) | Historically the emergency channel. Some legacy repeaters may still operate, but Ch 5 is the official channel. |

Call format: "Break, break, break — [Your Location] — Emergency — [Number of people] — [Nature of emergency] — Over."

See [Emergency Services](01-immediate/09-outback/emergency-services) for full details.

## Food

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| High-energy bars | Muesli bars, protein bars — 4+ per person per day |
| Nuts / trail mix | Calorie dense, long shelf life |
| Jerky / biltong | Protein, no cooking needed |
| Dried fruit | Energy, vitamins |
| Biscuits / crackers | Dry, long lasting |
| Instant noodles / soup | Only if you have surplus water for cooking |
| Hard lollies / sugar | Quick energy boost |
| Multivitamins | 1 week supply per person |

**Ration strategy:** In a survival situation, you do not need full nutrition — you need calories and water. Do not eat fatty or high-protein foods without sufficient water (digestion consumes water).

## Trip-Specific Add-Ons

| If crossing: | Add: |
|--------------|------|
| Sandy desert | Recovery tracks (Maxtrax or similar), tyre deflator, compressor, shovel |
| Inland rivers / creeks | Snorkel, wading gear, rope |
| Remote station country | Extra fuel (4× normal estimate), extra water, HF radio |
| Simpson Desert | 150 L+ fuel, 100 L+ water, 2 spare tyres, sand flag |
| Gibber plains | Tyre repair kit (sharp rocks), extra spares |

## Loading Checklist

Before every trip, tick these off:

- [ ] Water: 10 L per person per day + 2 extra days — **count the litres**
- [ ] PLB registered and in-date battery
- [ ] UHF radio works — test before departure
- [ ] Spare tyre(s) inflated and tread OK
- [ ] Toolkit present — open and check, don't just look
- [ ] Paper maps for your entire route
- [ ] First aid kit complete — no expired items
- [ ] Someone knows your route and ETA — written, not verbal
- [ ] Emergency contacts written down (paper, not just phone)
- [ ] Sun protection: hat, long sleeves, sunscreen, sunglasses
- [ ] Fire starters: at least two methods
