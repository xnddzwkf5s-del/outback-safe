# Outback 4WD Survival

Australia's remote outback is one of the most unforgiving environments on earth. When your vehicle breaks down on a station track, a desert crossing, or an unsealed road 200 km from the nearest town, the difference between survival and tragedy comes down to preparation, decision-making, and understanding how heat kills.

This section covers everything specific to vehicle-based outback survival: whether to stay with your vehicle or walk, how to survive extreme heat (35°C–50°C+), how to use your broken-down vehicle as a survival resource, and what to carry on every trip.

---

## Key Statistics

| Factor | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| Water requirement (extreme heat) | 8 L per person per day minimum |
| Time to death (45°C, no shade, no water) | < 24 hours |
| Time to death (45°C, shade, no water) | ~ 3 days |
| Typical response time (major roads) | 1–2 hours |
| Typical response time (remote tracks) | 4–24 hours |
| Extreme remote (RFDS airlift area) | 24–72 hours |
| Radiator water (without coolant) | ~ 10–20 L potential emergency supply |
| Black smoke signal visibility (burning tyre) | 30 km+ on clear day |
