Australia Overview Map Reference

Australia is the world's sixth-largest country by land area and the driest inhabited continent. The outback covers roughly 70% of the landmass — vast, arid, and sparsely populated. Understanding the geography, regions, and map conventions is essential before attempting any remote route.

Climate Zones

Australia's climate zones relevant to outback travel:

Zone Region Conditions Best Travel Window
Tropical Northern Australia (north of ~18°S) Wet season (Nov–Apr): cyclones, flooded roads, 90% humidity. Dry season (May–Oct): sunny, 20–30°C. May–October
Arid / Semi-Arid Interior desert regions Hot summers 35–50°C, cool winters 5–25°C (nights 0–5°C). Rain averages 150–350 mm/year, unpredictable. April–September
Temperate Southern Australia Mild winters, warm summers. Less extreme but remote routes still dangerous. Year-round (avoid extreme heat days)

Map Datum & Coordinate Systems

WGS84 (World Geodetic System 1984)

  • Standard for GPS: All consumer GPS devices default to WGS84
  • Australian equivalent: GDA94 (Geocentric Datum of Australia 1994) — functionally identical for outback navigation (sub-metre difference)
  • Always set GPS to WGS84 or GDA94 for all outback travel

AGD66 / AGD84 (Australian Geodetic Datum 1966/1984)

  • Warning: Older topographic maps (pre-2000) may use AGD66 or AGD84
  • AGD66 has an offset of approximately 200 metres east relative to WGS84/GDA94
  • The offset varies by location (average ~180–220 m across Australia)
  • 200 m in the outback can mean missing a well or fuel dump by a wide margin
  • Always check the datum stated on the map legend. If in doubt, assume AGD66 requires correction.

UTM Grid & MGA Zones

Australia spans MGA (Map Grid of Australia) zones 49 through 56:

  • Zone 49: ~114°E to ~120°E (Perth, south-west WA)
  • Zone 50: ~120°E to ~126°E (Kalgoorlie, central WA)
  • Zone 51: ~126°E to ~132°E (Laverton, Eucla)
  • Zone 52: ~132°E to ~138°E (Alice Springs, Coober Pedy)
  • Zone 53: ~138°E to ~144°E (Adelaide, Broken Hill)
  • Zone 54: ~144°E to ~150°E (Brisbane, Sydney)
  • Zone 55: ~150°E to ~156°E (Cape York, Cairns)
  • Zone 56: ~156°E to ~162°E (extreme north-east QLD)

All GPS units can be set to display UTM coordinates in the appropriate zone. For long east-west routes (Canning Stock Route, Anne Beadell), you will need to change zones mid-trip.

Essential Topographic Map Series

Series Scale Coverage Details
Geoscience Australia 1:250,000 1:250K (1 cm = 2.5 km) Entire continent, 546 sheets Standard reference. Covers entire country. Each sheet ~180 km east-west.
Geoscience Australia 1:100,000 1:100K (1 cm = 1 km) Selected areas, ~3,000 sheets Greater detail for route planning. Not all remote areas covered.
Hema Regional Desert Series Custom scales Simpson, Canning, Anne Beadell, etc. Purpose-designed for outback tracks. Fuel stop markers, GPS waypoints, notes.
Hema Great Desert Tracks Atlases 1:250K / 1:500K All major desert tracks Spiral-bound atlas format. Most popular choice for outback travellers.
Westprint Maps Various SA, NT, WA desert areas, Canning Stock Route Excellent detail. Water points, well information, historical notes.
State 1:50,000 1:50K (1 cm = 500 m) Limited coverage Only near populated areas. Not available for most remote tracks.

Recommended Map Priorities

  1. Hema Great Desert Tracks Atlas Set for the regions you plan to traverse
  2. Geoscience Australia 1:250,000 topographic sheets for primary navigation
  3. Westprint maps (Canning, Simpson, Anne Beadell — they are the gold standard for these)
  4. Digital maps loaded onto GPS (Avenza Maps with GEOpdf downloads, Hema Explorer app)
  5. Carry paper backups — devices fail, screens break, batteries drain
