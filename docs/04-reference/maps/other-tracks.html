Other Significant Outback Tracks

This page covers the remaining major outback tracks beyond the detailed route guides. Each entry provides key facts — distance, fuel stops, permits, and hazards — enough for trip planning reference.

Birdsville Track

Attribute Detail
Location Marree (SA) to Birdsville (QLD)
Distance 517 km
Surface Graded gravel and claypan, well maintained
Typical duration 1–2 days
Best season April–October

The Birdsville Track is SA's most famous outback route, running north through the Lake Eyre basin from Marree to Birdsville. It follows an old stock route with artesian mound springs along the way.

Waypoint Distance from Marree Notes
Marree 0 km Starting point. Historic town. Fuel, supplies.
Mungerannie Hotel 210 km Only fuel stop on the track. Pub, camping, basic supplies. Cold beer.
The Bubbler ~320 km Artesian bore. Hot water. Can fill containers.
Birdsville 517 km End. Fuel, pub, store, bakery.

Fuel: Marree and Birdsville (full), Mungerannie (midpoint, limited hours, expensive). Permits: None required. Public road. Hazards: Claypans impassable when wet. Dust in dry conditions. Road trains. Cross-reference: Simpson Desert — Birdsville is the eastern end of the Simpson Desert crossing.

Vehicle & Camping

Vehicle Requirements: High-clearance 2WD OK in dry weather. 4WD recommended. LT all-terrain tyres, 1 spare. Fuel range 550 km minimum. Snorkel not required. Standard recovery gear (snatch strap, shovel). 5L water/person/day. UHF radio recommended. No permits required.

Campsites: Marree → Mungerannie Hotel (~210 km, pub/camping/basic supplies, GPS: -27.9720, 138.6320) → The Bubbler bore (~320 km, bush camping only, GPS: -27.4400, 138.9000) → Birdsville (517 km, end, caravan park). No booking required for bush camps. Mungerannie can fill water containers.

Oodnadatta Track

Attribute Detail
Location Marree (SA) to Marla (SA)
Distance 620 km
Surface Graded gravel, generally good condition
Typical duration 2 days
Best season April–October

The Oodnadatta Track follows the historic route of the old Ghan railway, passing the ruins of the original railway sidings, artesian mound springs, and the unique Lake Eyre landscape.

Waypoint Distance from Marree Notes
Marree 0 km Start. Fuel, supplies.
William Creek ~200 km Pub, fuel, camping. Scenic flights over Lake Eyre.
Oodnadatta ~330 km Historic town. Fuel (24 hr card bowser), pub, camping. Pink Roadhouse.
Marla ~620 km End. Stuart Highway. Fuel, motel.

Highlights: Lake Eyre (south view via Halligan Bay turnoff), Coward Springs (natural thermal springs), Curdimurka (old Ghan ruins), Algebuckina Bridge (Australia's longest railway bridge — ghost town nearby). Fuel: Marree, William Creek, Oodnadatta, Marla. Good coverage. Permits: None required. Hazards: Heat in summer, road trains, dust. Generally an easy track.

Vehicle & Camping

Vehicle Requirements: High-clearance 2WD OK. 4WD recommended for side trips. LT all-terrain tyres, 1 spare. Fuel range 400 km minimum (frequent fuel stops). Standard recovery gear. 3L water/person/day. UHF helpful. No permits.

Campsites: Marree → William Creek (~200 km, pub/camping, GPS: -28.9120, 136.3440) → Oodnadatta (~330 km, Pink Roadhouse/camping/fuel, GPS: -27.5500, 135.4470) → Coward Springs (thermal pools/camping, GPS: -28.9700, 136.0800). Generally an easy 2-day run.

Strzelecki Track

Attribute Detail
Location Lyndhurst (SA) to Innamincka (SA)
Distance 460 km
Surface Graded gravel, maintained for gas field access
Typical duration 1–2 days
Best season April–October

The Strzelecki Track runs from the Flinders Ranges north-east to Innamincka on the Cooper Creek. Originally a stock route, it's now used extensively by the Moomba natural gas fields. Expect road trains.

Waypoint Distance from Lyndhurst Notes
Lyndhurst 0 km Fuel, supplies.
Moomba ~250 km Gas processing plant. No public services.
Innamincka 460 km Fuel, pub, camping, general store.
Cameron Corner ~600 km (via side track) Junction of SA/NSW/QLD borders.

Fuel: Lyndhurst and Innamincka. Moomba has a 24 hr card bowser for emergencies (limited hours). Gap is ~460 km — carry extra. Permits: Innamincka Regional Reserve permits may apply for camping. Hazards: Heavy road train traffic (Moomba gas field), sharp gravel, blacksoil when wet. Cross-reference: Birdsville Track — East of the Birdsville Track.

Vehicle & Camping

Vehicle Requirements: 4WD recommended (sharp gravel, road trains). LT all-terrain or light truck tyres, 1+ spare. Fuel range 500 km minimum (one 460 km gap). Standard recovery gear. Watch for Moomba gas field road trains. 5L water/person/day. UHF essential for road train communication. Innamincka Regional Reserve permits may apply for camping.

Campsites: Lyndhurst → Moomba (~250 km, no public services, emergency fuel only) → Innamincka (460 km, pub/camping/fuel, GPS: -27.7100, 140.7390). Side trip: Cameron Corner (~600 km, three-state border junction, camping, GPS: -26.9900, 141.0030). Bush camping along the track.

Tanami Track

Attribute Detail
Location Alice Springs (NT) to Halls Creek (WA)
Distance 1,030 km
Surface Gravel, claypan, sandstone. Variable condition.
Typical duration 3–4 days
Best season April–September

The Tanami is an important freight route between the NT and WA, and the main connection from Alice Springs to the Canning Stock Route. Heavily used by road trains, mining trucks, and travellers.

Waypoint Distance from Alice Springs Notes
Alice Springs 0 km Full services.
Tilmouth Well ~250 km Roadhouse. Fuel, camping.
Rabbit Flat ~490 km Roadhouse. Only fuel stop on the Tanami proper. Expensive. Limited hours.
The Granites ~550 km Mining area. No public services.
Billiluna turnoff ~870 km Turnoff to Canning Stock Route north.
Halls Creek 1,030 km End. Full services.

Fuel: Alice Springs, Tilmouth Well, Rabbit Flat, Halls Creek. The gaps are long (~250 km between each). Carry 60 L+ extra. Permits: None for the road. Aboriginal land permits required for side trips off the Tanami. Hazards: Road trains (undulating road, blind crests), heat, wildlife (kangaroos at dusk), sharp rocks. Cross-reference: Canning Stock Route — The Tanami is the access route to the CSR's northern end.

Vehicle & Camping

Vehicle Requirements: 4WD or high-clearance AWD recommended. LT all-terrain or light truck tyres, 2 spares recommended. Fuel range 400 km between stops — carry 60 L+ extra. Snorkel recommended for dust. Heavy-duty suspension for corrugations. Standard recovery gear. 5L water/person/day. UHF radio (road train coms). Sat phone recommended for remote sections. Aboriginal land permits required for side trips.

Campsites: Alice Springs → Tilmouth Well (~250 km, roadhouse/camping, GPS: -22.8000, 131.2500) → Rabbit Flat (~490 km, roadhouse/ONLY fuel, GPS: -20.2000, 127.5000, limited hours) → Halls Creek (1,030 km, end, full services). Bush camping available at pull-offs. Beware road trains at all times.

Plenty Highway

Attribute Detail
Location Boulia (QLD) to Alice Springs (NT)
Distance 650 km
Surface Graded gravel, well maintained (west of QLD border)
Typical duration 1–2 days
Best season April–October

The Plenty Highway is a significant east-west link across the NT/QLD border country, connecting Boulia in the Channel Country to the Stuart Highway near Alice Springs.

Waypoint Distance from Boulia Notes
Boulia 0 km Fuel, supplies.
QLD/NT border ~120 km
Jervois Station ~250 km Fuel (limited, expensive), camping.
Atitjere ~250 km (alternate turn) Aboriginal community. Fuel.
Gem Tree ~330 km Roadhouse. Fuel.
Alice Springs 650 km End. Full services.

Fuel: Boulia, Jervois Station (or Atitjere), Alice Springs. The gap from Boulia to Jervois is ~250 km. Carry 40 L extra. Permits: None for the highway. Aboriginal land permits for side trips. Hazards: Heat, dust, road trains. Easy track when dry. Cross-reference: Simpson Desert — Boulia is the northern alternative route to the Simpson.

Vehicle & Camping

Vehicle Requirements: 4WD or high-clearance 2WD in dry weather. LT all-terrain tyres, 1 spare. Fuel range 300 km between stops — carry 40 L+ extra. Standard recovery gear. 3L water/person/day. UHF useful. No permits for the highway, side trips may need Aboriginal land permits.

Campsites: Boulia → Jervois Station (~250 km, fuel/camping, GPS: -22.9200, 136.2700) → Gem Tree Roadhouse (~330 km, fuel, GPS: -23.0000, 135.3500) → Alice Springs (650 km, end). Easy 1-2 day crossing when dry.

Gunbarrel Highway

Attribute Detail
Location Giles (WA) to Carnegie (WA)
Distance ~600 km
Surface Unmaintained desert track, sand dunes, rocky sections
Typical duration 4–5 days
Best season April–September

The original Len Beadell Gunbarrel Road Construction Party route, built in the 1950s–1960s to access the Woomera rocket range. The Gunbarrel Highway is now a challenging, remote desert track traversed by experienced 4WDers only.

Waypoint Notes
Giles Weather station. Fuel (limited, call ahead).
Victory Well Historic water point.
Vee Station Abandoned.
Well 33 (CSR) Connects to Canning Stock Route.
Carnegie Station End. Fuel (expensive, call ahead).

Fuel: Giles and Carnegie. That's it. Minimum 800 km range. Carry 100 L+ extra. Permits: WA DBCA permit for travel through nature reserves. Hazards: Extreme isolation, unmaintained dunes, spinifex punctures, no services. Cross-reference: Canning Stock Route, Anne Beadell Highway — Part of the Beadell network.

Vehicle & Camping

Vehicle Requirements: Heavy-duty 4WD MANDATORY. LT 10-ply tyres, 2+ spares. Fuel range 800 km minimum — carry 100 L+ extra. Snorkel essential (dust). Heavy-duty suspension, upgraded shocks. Winch, Maxtrax, exhaust jack, comprehensive recovery kit. 10L water/person/day × 5 days minimum. Sat phone essential. PLB mandatory. WA DBCA permit required.

Campsites: Giles (~250 km from start, limited fuel, GPS: -25.0340, 128.3010) → Victory Well (bush camp, historic water point, GPS: -25.2000, 126.8000) → Well 33 / Kunawarritji (CSR junction, fuel available, GPS: -22.8390, 125.7610) → Carnegie Station (end, expensive fuel, GPS: -25.8070, 122.9750). Bush camp only — no facilities anywhere on route.

Great Central Road

Attribute Detail
Location Laverton (WA) to Yulara (NT)
Distance ~1,100 km
Surface Graded gravel, generally well maintained
Typical duration 3–4 days
Best season April–September

The Great Central Road is the main east-west route through the southern half of Western Australia, linking Laverton to Yulara (Ayers Rock Resort). It crosses through Aboriginal lands, Ngaanyatjarra country, and the Warburton Ranges.

Waypoint Distance from Laverton Notes
Laverton 0 km Start. Full services.
Warburton ~400 km Aboriginal community. Fuel (limited, expensive).
Docker River ~700 km Aboriginal community. Fuel (limited).
Yulara (Ayers Rock) ~1,100 km End. Full services.

Fuel: Laverton, Warburton, Docker River, Yulara. Long gaps (~400 km Laverton to Warburton). Carry 80 L+ extra. Permits: Ngaanyatjarra Council transit permit required. Apply online or at Warburton. Allow 2–4 weeks. Hazards: Road trains, dust, Aboriginal community speed limits, heat. Graded road — generally easier than other desert tracks. Permit note: The transit permit is strictly enforced. Do not travel the Great Central Road without one.

Vehicle & Camping

Vehicle Requirements: High-clearance 4WD or AWD recommended. LT all-terrain tyres, 1+ spare. Fuel range 400 km minimum — carry 80 L+ extra. Snorkel recommended (dust). Standard suspension adequate (graded road). Standard recovery gear. 5L water/person/day. UHF radio helpful. Ngaanyatjarra Council transit permit REQUIRED — apply 2-4 weeks ahead. Strictly enforced — do not travel without permit.

Campsites: Laverton → Warburton (~400 km, community/fuel/supplies, GPS: -26.1280, 126.5820) → Docker River (~700 km, community/fuel, GPS: -24.8670, 129.1000) → Yulara / Ayers Rock Resort (1,100 km, end, full services). Bush camping along the road. Respect Aboriginal community speed limits.

Savannah Way

Attribute Detail
Location Cairns (QLD) to Broome (WA)
Distance ~3,700 km
Surface Mix of sealed, gravel, and 4WD sections
Typical duration 2–4 weeks
Best season May–October

The Savannah Way is Australia's northernmost across-the-top touring route, linking Cairns to Broome via the Gulf of Carpentaria, the Savannah Gulf region, and the Kimberley.

Major Stops State Notes
Cairns QLD Start. Full services.
Normanton QLD Gulf region. Fuel, pub.
Burketown QLD Sweat state — heat, humidity. Fuel.
Borroloola NT Fuel, camping.
Timber Creek NT Fuel, pub. Victoria River.
Kununurra WA Full services. Gibb River Road starts.
Broome WA End. Full services.

Fuel: Regular roadhouses. Gap from Normanton to Burketown ~200 km. Ensure 400 km range. Permits: None required for the route itself. National park camping fees apply. Hazards: Wet season (Nov–Apr): road closures, flooding, cyclone risk. Crocodiles in all northern waterways. Heat and humidity (35–40°C, 80%+ humidity). Cross-reference: Gibb River Road, Cape York — The Savannah Way connects both regions.

Vehicle & Camping

Vehicle Requirements: High-clearance 2WD OK on sealed sections. 4WD recommended for gravel sections and side tracks. LT all-terrain tyres, 1 spare. Fuel range 400 km minimum. Standard recovery gear. 5L water/person/day (hot and humid). UHF helpful. No permits for the route. Crocodile safety: do not camp within 50 m of waterways.

Campsites: Cairns → Normanton (~600 km, pub/camping, GPS: -17.6680, 141.0800) → Burketown (~800 km, pub/camping, GPS: -17.7410, 139.5450) → Borroloola (~1,200 km, fuel/camping, GPS: -16.0710, 136.3040) → Timber Creek (~2,000 km, fuel/pub/camping, GPS: -15.6550, 130.4800) → Kununurra (~2,200 km, full services, GPS: -15.7730, 128.7390) → Broome (3,700 km, end). Multiple accommodation options along the way — mix of caravan parks, pub stays, and bush camps.

Kidson Track

Attribute Detail
Location Western Australia. Telfer (east) to near Marble Bar (west)
Distance ~400 km
Surface Rough, unmaintained, multiple creek crossings, dunes
Typical duration 5–7 days
Best season May–August
Difficulty Extreme — professionals only

The Kidson Track is one of the most remote and least-travelled tracks in Australia. It runs through the western Great Sandy Desert between the Telfer gold mine and the Marble Bar region. The Kidson is not a public road — travel is unmaintained and extremely isolated.

Hazard Detail
Isolation Extreme. Days without seeing another vehicle.
Track condition Unmaintained. Overgrown sections, fallen trees, dune crossings.
Water None. Carry ALL water.
Fuel None. Carry everything. Minimum 1,000 km range.
Permits WA DBCA permit required. Aboriginal land permits.
Communications Sat phone essential. PLB mandatory.

Recommendation: The Kidson Track should only be attempted by experienced outback travellers in well-prepared vehicles (2+ vehicles). This is not a route for first-time desert crossers. Cross-reference: Canning Stock Route — The Kidson is sometimes combined with a northern CSR variation.

Vehicle & Camping

Vehicle Requirements: EXTREME — heavy-duty 4WD only. LT 10-ply mud-terrain, 3+ spares. Fuel range 1,000 km minimum — carry ALL fuel. Snorkel essential. Heavy-duty suspension with upgraded shocks. Full recovery kit: winch, Maxtrax, exhaust jack, snatch straps. 10L water/person/day × 7 days minimum — no water anywhere. Sat phone essential, PLB mandatory. WA DBCA and Aboriginal land permits required. Recommended: 2+ vehicles. NOT for beginners.

Campsites: Bush camp only — no facilities, no wells, no services anywhere on the Kidson Track. Plan 5-7 nights of self-sufficient bush camping. Telfer area (east, GPS: -21.7200, 122.2100) → mid-route (GPS: -21.5000, 121.0000) → Marble Bar area (west, GPS: -21.1500, 119.7400). Carry ALL supplies for entire trip. Nearest help is days away.
