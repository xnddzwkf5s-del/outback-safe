Weapons

Weapons serve three purposes in a survival context: hunting (acquiring food), defense (protecting your group and resources), and trade (valuable barter goods). Mastery of weapon-making is one of the most leverageable skills in a long-term collapse scenario — a competent weaponsmith can trade for food, shelter, and protection.

Safety First

Never point a weapon at anything you do not intend to destroy. Store weapons and ammunition separately. Train every member of your group in weapon safety before they touch one.

Legal Note

ℹ️ Information
This content is provided for **educational purposes only**, particularly for survival scenarios where normal legal frameworks have collapsed. Manufacturing firearms and explosives is heavily regulated in most jurisdictions. Consult local laws before engaging in any weapons manufacture.

Progression of Technology

Weapons technology follows a predictable path that can be re-traced in a survival scenario:

Stage Technology Time to Master Key Materials
1 — Stone Clubs, spears, slings, atlatls 1–4 weeks Wood, stone, sinew
2 — Medieval Bows, crossbows, swords, armour 3–12 months Iron, steel, leather
3 — Gunpowder Black powder, smoothbores, flintlocks 6–24 months Saltpetre, sulfur, iron

Start with stage 1 even if you plan to advance. Stone-age weapons are immediately achievable, require no industrial base, and the skills transfer forward. A group that can reliably hunt with bows will not starve while waiting for gunpowder production.

Choosing Your Path

Your weapon development path depends on available resources:

  • Abundant hardwood, limited metal → Focus on bows and crossbows (Medieval section)
  • Access to iron ore and a forge → Swords, spearheads, and eventually firearms
  • Bat guano or manure deposits → Accelerate toward gunpowder production
  • Defensive position with high ground → Prioritise fortification first, weapons second
ℹ️ Information
**AU/CN/SEA Note:** Bamboo is an excellent weapon material available throughout Southeast Asia, southern China, and northern Australia. Bamboo makes excellent arrow shafts, spear hafts, bow backing, and can even be used for improvised firearms barrels in a pinch. See the Medieval section for specific bamboo weapon techniques.
